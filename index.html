Senior Design Project

University of New Mexico
Department of Chemical and Biological Engineering



Project Objective

  • Develop a system that will:
    • Remove all viruses and bacteria from water including:
      • Cryptosporidium
      • Giardia
      • Escherichia coli
    • Remove all non biological contaminants including:
      • Arsenic
      • copper
      • lead
    • Produce 1,000,000 gallons of clean water every day
    • Provide cheap water for use of residents of Flint, Michigan
    • Reduced price to help low income families in Flint


Block Flow Diagram

Below is a block flow diagram of the water purification process. The process was designed into three main stages: Pre-Treatment, Filtration, and Disinfection.

The first stage is Pre-Treatment which includes coagulation/flocculation and settling. The engineering parameters that were investigated included what type of propeller was going to be used in coagulation, how fast the propeller would have to move into the tank to achieve particle contact, the flow rate of the flocculant to be added, and the tank size.

The second stage in the process of filtration. Our main filtration unit was a Reverse Osmosis filter. The pore size, pressure requirments, pipe length, and pipe diameter were investigated when designing our process. We had other filters in our process including the reapid sand filter and activated carbon filter. The water softening stage with also included here because it operates as filter essentially. Its main function is to remove calcium and magnesium for the source.

The third stage of the process was disinfection. This included chlorination and ultraviolet disinfection. You may ask why there are two units for disinfection. That is because certain bacteria is chlorine resistant. By adding UV disinfection we are ensuring that our water is safe for consumptive use.
